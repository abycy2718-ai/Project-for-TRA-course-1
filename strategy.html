Revision & Translation Strategy

Explaining the rationale behind the Hong Kong leaflet

Structural Strategy

I begin by introducing Hong Kong in general, before moving on to how B+H achieved success supported by three key aspects:

  • Growth from a single employee to over 20 architects in Hong Kong;
  • Leadership in major Greater Bay Area projects;
  • Integration into the Surbana Jurong (SJ) Group, a global leader in urban solutions.

As highlighted on B+H’s official website: “B+H is a member of the Surbana Jurong (SJ) Group, a global leader in urban solutions ranked 14th in the World Architecture 100 for 2024.”

This underscores the firm’s position under the umbrella of a global industry giant.

Scope and Selection

Due to word constraints, other aspects of B+H’s story as an inspiration for business growth in Hong Kong are not included. For example, individuals such as Andrew Humphreys are omitted, since these names might be less meaningful to investors outside the architecture sector.

Likewise, the forward-looking potential of cultural landscape design in Shenzhen is not addressed, as it may be too specialized for a general business audience.

Translation Considerations

For the Chinese translation, I aimed for naturalness while retaining professionalism. Following Katharina Reiß’s text type categorization, I incorporated an operative dimension: adjusting sentence structures, replacing one comma with exclamation marks, and adding subtle personification to align more closely with natural Chinese usage.

In my view, the informative and expressive functions remain broadly consistent with that of the original English text.
